**Problem:** The review page is the last thing someone sees before they hand over their payment details, and because most of Perfect Pet's sales come through aggregators, it was usually the first time that person had met the brand at all. So it had to explain the product and earn trust at the same time, for someone with no reason yet to believe it. The Hotjar recordings showed people sitting on the page for five minutes or more, opening policy PDFs to make sense of it, and a fair few leaving to check something on the main site and never coming back.

**Approach:** I started with a competitor review, watched Hotjar recordings of people using the live page, and ran 11 interviews with colleagues from across the business. Recruiting internally was something the timeline forced on me, and the usual wisdom says people who work in pet insurance make poor stand-in users. By the middle of the first interview I realised I had that backwards. These were the people who handle the complaints and the declined claims, so they knew exactly what happens when the page leaves someone with a rosier impression of their cover than the policy can actually back up.

**Constraints:** A single two-week sprint. Everything had to be buildable in the MudBlazor component framework, and every line of the existing policy copy had to stay exactly where it was for FCA compliance reasons. I couldn't simplify the words, so I had to work on how they were shown.

**Outcome:** I rebuilt the hierarchy of the page so the information people actually needed at that moment came forward, and the wall of policy text stopped being the first thing they hit. What started as a cosmetic refresh turned into a clarity-and-trust problem, aimed at the exact spot in the funnel where confident buyers were quietly giving up.
