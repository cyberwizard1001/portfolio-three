**Problem:** Most of the work on this site points outwards, at problems other people had. This one points at me. The portfolio itself has turned out to be the longest-running and most stubborn design problem I've got, because I've redesigned it six times and rebuilt it three. Every time I get most of the way there, then start arguing with myself about whether any of it is right.

**Approach:** I named the pattern, because once you do you can at least tell when it's happening. For the first couple of weeks I design for myself and I like it. Then the question quietly changes from "does this feel like me?" to "what will other people think?", and I start designing for an imaginary sceptical crowd instead. From there I either strip it back to something austere or pile on more to prove I have taste. Both are just fear wearing different outfits.

**Constraints:** My own doubt, mostly, plus the tools. Two of these sites were built in Flutter, which paints onto a canvas, so basic web behaviours like selecting text or trusting the back button stopped being free. I ended up with a 10k-line portfolio and evenings spent wiring up workarounds, which is not where a designer's attention should go.

**Outcome:** The current site is portfolio_two rebuilt from scratch in Astro and plain HTML and CSS, a Linux-desktop theme that leans into where my UX story actually started. Going back to basics felt almost embarrassingly freeing. The thing I'd defend most is doing it badly in public rather than waiting until I felt sure, because I have never once felt sure, and the doubt isn't a bug in the process. It's the part where I care.
